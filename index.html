THE CONCEPT

Two games.
One battlefield.

Checkker merges the strategic depth of chess with the hand-building tension of poker. Every card you draw dictates which piece you can move. Every capture feeds your poker score pile. The result is a game where tactical brilliance and calculated risk collide on every single turn.

HOW TO PLAY

Master the fusion

Every turn is a decision. Three cards, unlimited possibilities.

01

Draw Your Hand

At the start of each turn, draw up to 3 cards from the deck. Your first turn gives you 3 fresh cards. Each subsequent turn replenishes back to 3.

02

Choose & Play a Card

Select one card from your hand to move the corresponding chess piece. A King card moves your King, a Jack moves a Knight, 3-9 move Pawns. The Ace is wild—it moves anything.

03

Move Your Piece

Make a legal chess move with the piece type matching your played card. Standard chess rules apply—the board, the pieces, the strategy. The card just decides which piece you command.

04

Capture & Score

If your move captures an opponent's piece, the played card goes to your score pile (face-up). No capture? Card is discarded. Captures fuel your poker hands—the aggressive player builds the best hand.

05

Win the War

The game ends on checkmate, draw, time-out, or deck exhaustion. Both players then score: Chess result + best poker hands from your score pile = Total. Highest total wins. Even a lost chess position can be redeemed by superior captures.
